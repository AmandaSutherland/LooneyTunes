Below are our CAD render pictures from initial ideation to final product.

This is our first CAD of our idea of what our winding mechanism should be like. We decided to go through with a worm gear so that the shaft does not slip.

This is our next prototype of our winding mechanism. There's an additional smaller gear at the bottom of the worm gear so that there is more stability in the worm gear and the larger gear. We've also added the encoder and the wood for the pulling mechanism. There's also an additional key for the large gear; this is so that there is more contact between the large gear and the shaft.

This is our overall body of our string instrument. There's four of our winding mechanisms included and our bridge.

This is our first assembly of the mechanical system. Our bow is just a thick piece of wood at this point.

This is our final assembly of the mechanical system. We've replaced the bow with an acrylic wheel because the original idea of having horsehair on the wood wheel did not work as intended. The acrylic material ended up working as well as normal violin horsehair bows, so we decided to go with the acrylic wheel. There is also additional backing support so the back and the bridge do not bend as much when the strings are in tension.
